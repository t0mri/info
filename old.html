Approach

When faced with a problem, I first ask if it’s necessary to solve. Often, the problem is unnecessary. For example, in my latest project, I had to check if hero coors already existed in my data. Instead of finding a fast algorithm to iterate, I changed the data structure, giving linear time complexity.

I’m a great fan of shifting perspectives and I always try to do that so that I can achieve something that ordinary can’t. A simple example would be this portfolio itself. I didn’t make my portfolio just as a showcase, I want you to know who I am and what makes me special. So, instead of looking at top dev portfolios, I looked at how movie directors present their ideas. That’s how I got this theme and design.

Whenever I come up with an idea, I usually implement it in two different ways. First, I write it to work as soon as possible, but it is not reusable or modular. Once I make the whole thing work, I write the finest, robust, and scalable code of the same algorithm, That scales with ease
